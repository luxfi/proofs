\subsection*{Warp 2.0 PQ soundness}

\begin{theorem}[Warp 2.0 envelope unforgeability]
\label{thm:warp-v2-soundness}
Let $\mathsf{env}_2$ be a Warp 2.0 envelope
(Definition~\ref{def:warp-v2-envelope}) with successful verification
under $\mathsf{Verify}_{V2}$. Then forging an alternative $\mathsf{env}'_2$
on the same $(\mathsf{sourceChainID}, \mathsf{sourceNebulaRoot},
\mathsf{sourceKeyEraID}, \mathsf{sourceGeneration})$ tuple with a
different $\mathsf{payload}$ requires breaking either:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
  \item BLS12-381 co-CDH (forging the Beam component); OR
  \item Module-LWE / Module-SIS (forging the Pulsar Pulse,
        Theorem~\ref{thm:pulsar-suf-cma}).
\end{enumerate}
A quantum adversary breaking only co-CDH (and not MLWE/MSIS) succeeds
against the Beam alone; the Pulse remains unforgeable, so the v2
envelope as a whole remains sound under $\mathsf{Verify}_{V2}$.

\textbf{PQ finality lives in the Pulse, not in any optional Groth16
wrapper.} If the envelope additionally carries a Z-Chain Groth16 proof
of the ML-DSA cert set, that proof is a classical compatibility /
compression artifact and does not contribute to the PQ reduction
(Remark~\ref{rem:groth16-wrapper}).
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Warp Private orthogonality]
$\mathsf{Warp\,Private}$ (FHE-encrypted payload, optionally
Pulse-signed) is orthogonal to $\mathsf{Warp\,2.0}$. Warp Private
provides confidentiality; Warp 2.0 provides PQ finality. They compose:
a Warp Private envelope MAY carry a Pulse, in which case Theorem
\ref{thm:warp-v2-soundness} applies unchanged to the encrypted-payload
binding.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Future PQ-friendly proof system]
The current Z-Chain rollup uses Groth16 for ML-DSA cert-set
compression; a future $\mathsf{Warp\,Private\,PQ}$ replaces the
pairing-based SNARK with a STARK or lattice-based transparent proof
system. Until that lands, do not classify the Groth16-wrapped lane as
post-quantum.
\end{remark}
