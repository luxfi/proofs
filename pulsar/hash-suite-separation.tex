\subsection*{Hash-suite domain separation}

\begin{theorem}[Cross-suite signatures do not collide]
\label{thm:hash-suite-separation}
Let $\sigma_{\textsc{sha3}}$ and $\sigma_{\textsc{blake3}}$ be Pulsar
signatures produced under
$\mathsf{HashSuiteID} = \mathsf{Pulsar\text{-}SHA3}$ and
$\mathsf{HashSuiteID} = \mathsf{Pulsar\text{-}BLAKE3}$ respectively,
on otherwise identical input. Then
$\mathsf{Pulsar.Verify}_{\mathsf{Pulsar\text{-}SHA3}}(
\mathsf{GroupKey}, m, \sigma_{\textsc{blake3}}) = 0$
and symmetrically for the other direction, with overwhelming
probability over the verifier's hash function.

The separation is ensured by binding $\mathsf{HashSuiteID}$ into
the transcript via the TupleHash personalization
(Definition~\ref{def:pulsar-transcript}); a cross-suite forgery
would constitute a collision on the personalized TupleHash, which is
collision-resistant under the underlying SHA3/BLAKE3 sponge.
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Era-pinning]
The $\mathsf{HashSuiteID}$ is recorded at $\mathsf{Pulsar.Bootstrap}$
and remains immutable through every $\mathsf{Reshare}$ within an era.
Reanchor MAY change the suite for the new era. This pinning prevents
``split-brain'' scenarios where activation verifies under one suite
and routine signing under another.
\end{remark}
